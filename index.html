About Research Projects

Hi, I'm Linda!

I'm currently a second year MASc graduate student in the Vision and Image Processing Lab at the University of Waterloo, supervised by Prof. Alexander Wong. For my undergraduate degree, I studied Systems Design Engineering at the University of Waterloo.

I'm interested in artificial intelligence, particularly, computer vision and deep learning. My research focus is in combining different visual perception cues, such as object detection and depth estimation, to build a rich scene understanding. My goal is to contribute to the advancement of AI by applying my research to areas such as AI for accessibility and autonomous driving.

In addition to my interest in AI, I am also interested in teaching. I have been a teaching assistant for Data Structures and Algorithms and am currently a teaching assistant for Digital Computation (C++).

Through the co-op program at the University of Waterloo, I have had 6 internships ranging from hardware engineering to research to software engineering. My most recent internships were at Lyft Level 5 as a software engineer on the Perception team, where I worked on monocular depth estimation. My work is highlighted here. At Facebook, I was a software engineer on the Computational Photography team, where I worked on augmented reality.
